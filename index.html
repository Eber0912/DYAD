Do you need your foundation repaired?

13 Signs that you need foundation repair or replacement

  1. Cracks in Brick Facing
  2. Chimney Cracking or Leaning
  3. Drywall Cracks
  4. Sinking Foundation
  5. Uneven or Sticking Doors or Windows
  6. Large gaps in window and door frames
  7. Interior plaster walls cracked
  8. Walls are beginning to lean noticeably
  9. Window and/or door trim are developing spaces
  10. Floors are starting to settle and become uneven
  11. Foundations are sinking
  12. Cracks found on foundations,
    basements, slabs, and/or concretes walls
  13. Stair step & horizontal cracks in the mortar joints

Do you notice doors that won’t close? Bulging floors? Perhaps you see cracks in your walls or floor.
These are indications of foundation distress,
which may require a Built Strong foundation repair.

One reason why a foundation may fail is
gains or losses in soil moisture causing swelling or
shrinking of the soil; another could be poor building
pad construction (soils were not compacted properly).
Both these conditions disturb a foundation
causing movement. If you see signs of a foundation
distress, don’t delay in solving the problem,
as it will lead to further damage to your property.
Built Strong’s team of experts can help you with a swift, quality repair that will address any issues to your foundation.

Services

Complete Foundation Replacement:
If necessary we can perform a complete foundation replacement. This would include replacing the entire concrete slab under the home and pouring a new layer of concrete which would be reinforced with rebar technology.

Foundation Repair
Basic repair needs include solutions for issues such as cracks in your walls or floors.

Square Shaft Pile Helical Piers: Used in both uplift and compression situations.
These piles have considerable weight bearing capacity. However a majority projects with Square
Shaft piles are used for upward or tension jobs intended to
raise the

With the type of fitting process that we use, the Helical Piers
can be installed any time of the year or any season and only

Jacks: Multiple different sized jacks may be used to correct Foundation leveling issues. The jacks are used to lift raised foundations by disconnecting the house from the foundation frame and manually jacking up the house then reattaching the house
to the foundation framing. Each jack has a capacity of supporting
up to 20 tons or 40,000 pounds.

Concrete Slab repair: Concrete slabs often crack after a
period of time. Slab settlement and cracking is often due to
soil related issues. Often times intense weather conditions
such as heat which can cause the soils to shrink, affecting
levels of foundations. If left untreated, cracks can increase in
size, causing major damage to your home. A cracked slab can
eventually pull apart walls, cause drainage problems, and
even allow insects and vermin to enter the home. Built
Strong’s quick and easy foundation slab repairs will prevent
any of these unnecessary and irritating problems.

Slab Repair Solutions
Slab issues are resolved by slab piers. Slab piers are a posts which provide resistance, adding support for the concrete.
Slab piers are driven onto the ground and then are
used to lift the foundation slab.

Benefits to Slab Piers:
- Year round applications
- Can improve or increase home value
- Fats installation
- No damage to surrounding areas and yards

Temporary techniques used during projects
We also use temporary shoring.
Temporary shoring is a
temporary support system either lumber or steel used to
hold the structure during repairs preventing collapse.
